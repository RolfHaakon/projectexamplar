About Us


Townsville Community Music Centre is a non-profit incorporated organisation, serving musicians and their audience in North Queensland. It presents concerts and workshops throughout the year, featuring touring artists, locally-based professional, and emerging artists. They showcase a diverse range of genres including classical, jazz, folk, blues, world, and contemporary music.


History

  1. 1983

    The Townsville Community Music Centre was established on May 24th. Over the next few weeks, a constitution was drafted and endorsed at another meeting on June 15th. On September 1983, Fred Thompson was made chairman of the centre, with Gordon Dean as his Deputy and Jan Eagleton as his Secretary

  2. 1984

    The centre recruited Mira Henderson as their first staff member, taking up the Acting Director Position. She instigated a series of classes starting in 1984 using the skills of people living in Townsville to enhance and encourage music learners of all ages.

  3. 1985

    Kirsty Veron was appointed as the first Director, and worked with Mira in the 1983 classes. She held this position until 1988 leading the new organisation into a strong position in the Townsville arts community.

  4. 1985

    Also in 1985, the Music Centre produced Benjamin Britten's Noyes Fludde in St James Anglican Cathedral involving hundreds of children and some of Townsville's best-known musicians. This was by the no means the only major production mounted by the Music Centre but was probably the largest.

  5. 1987

    The centre had its first fund raising dinner this year, providing a wonderful mixture of food, wine, and music. The first few dinners were held at the home of Paul and June Tonnoir in North Ward. The dinner became an annual tradition which ran until 2004.

  6. 1988

    In 1988, Mary Lou Schoenfeldt was appointed as Administrator. Although this was initially a small part time job with limited responsibilities, Mary Lou contributed tremendously to the Centre, and the cultural life of the City. Unfortunately, she retired in 2003 due to ill health.

  7. 1990

    This year the first lunchtime concert was held at the PercTucker Gallery. The importance of providing performance opportunities for emerging musicians had become evident. Lunchtime concert continued for a number of years as one of the major activities of the Centre.

  8. 1999

    Arts Queensland, a major funder of Music Centre, agreed to provide funding on a three year basis. This made it easier for the Centre to plan and reduced the time spent on repetitive grant applications. This arrangement ran until 2004.

  9. 2003

    A spectacular variety concert was held at the Townsville Civic Theatre to celebrate twenty years of the Music Centre and as a tribute to May Lou.

  10. 2005

    The Centre moved to offices in the Old Magistrate's Court. More details of the period 1983 - 2008 can be found in the book, "Townsville Community Music Centre: some memories of the first 25 years" written by Jean Dartnall. (Copies available from the Music Centre).

  11. 2012

    The Centre moved to its present office in the Townsville Civic Theatre building.
